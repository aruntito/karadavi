# Machine Trust

Machine trust describes how increasingly autonomous systems evaluate credibility, authority, reliability, and contextual integrity across digital environments.

As AI-native interfaces become primary discovery layers, trust formation increasingly depends on structured signals interpretable by machine systems rather than purely human-facing presentation.

---

## Key Areas

- semantic consistency
- entity continuity
- contextual integrity
- citation relationships
- information reliability
- machine-readable authority
- distributed trust signals
- knowledge graph alignment

---

## Observation

The future of visibility increasingly depends on whether systems can interpret an entity as coherent, trustworthy, and contextually reliable across fragmented information environments.
