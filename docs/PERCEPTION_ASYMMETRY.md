# Perception Asymmetry

Perception asymmetry describes the growing gap between how humans perceive information and how machine systems interpret, prioritize, and contextualize it.

As AI-native interfaces increasingly mediate discovery, entities may appear highly visible to human audiences while remaining structurally weak within machine-readable ecosystems, or vice versa.

---

## Core Areas

- machine-human interpretation gaps
- semantic visibility imbalance
- contextual prioritization
- retrieval asymmetry
- machine-native discoverability
- distributed trust divergence
- informational interpretation layers
- perception coordination systems

---

## Observation

Future digital influence may increasingly depend on aligning human-facing narratives with machine-readable contextual integrity across interconnected intelligence systems.
